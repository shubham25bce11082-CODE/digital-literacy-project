# Case Study: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a rapidly growing cybercrime in India, where attackers trick users into authorizing fraudulent transactions. This type of crime usually happens through social engineering techniques.

A common scenario begins when a fraudster contacts the victim via phone, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or even a buyer/seller from an online marketplace.Always use a 
trust worthy app to do online payments.
## How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. The attacker gains the victim’s trust by sounding professional and creating urgency.
2. They send a “collect request” or ask the victim to scan a QR code.
3. They falsely claim this is required to receive money.
4. The victim enters their UPI PIN.
5. Instead of receiving money, the victim unknowingly authorizes a payment.

## Targeted Victims
Targets are typically individuals unfamiliar with digital payment systems, elderly users, or people urgently expecting money. However, even educated users fall victim due to convincing tactics.

## Consequences
Victims may lose significant amounts of money within seconds, often with little chance of recovery. These incidents also cause emotional stress, loss of trust in digital systems, and financial instability.

## Conclusion
This case highlights the importance of awareness: users should never share their UPI PIN, approve unknown requests, or scan QR codes to receive money. Cyber awareness is the strongest defense against such fraud. always be careful while doing online transactions and never trust blindly on anyone.
