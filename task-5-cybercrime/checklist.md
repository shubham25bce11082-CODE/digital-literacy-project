# Stay Safe Online – Checklist for College Students 

## Prevention Tips
- Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or passwords with anyone—even if they claim to be from your bank.
- Do not scan QR codes to receive money; scanning a QR code always initiates a payment.
- Always verify “collect requests” before approving them in apps like Google Pay, PhonePe, or Paytm.
- Enable two-factor authentication (2FA) on all important accounts (email, banking, social media).
- Avoid using public Wi-Fi for banking or payments; use mobile data or a secure network instead.
- Check website URLs carefully before logging in—look for “https://” and avoid suspicious links.
- Keep your apps and phone updated to protect against security vulnerabilities.
- Do not click on unknown links or download attachments from strangers.
- Log out of accounts on shared or public devices.
- Regularly monitor your bank statements and transaction history.

## If You Are a Victim
- Immediately report the fraud at: https://cybercrime.gov.in
- Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930
- Inform your bank as soon as possible to block further transactions.

# Reflection 

While researching cybercrime, what surprised me the most was how simple tricks can lead to serious financial loss. Many scams do not rely on advanced hacking but instead exploit human trust and lack of awareness. I realized that even educated people can fall victim if they act in a hurry or without verifying details.

One habit I will personally change is being more cautious before approving any payment request or clicking on links. I will always double-check the source and purpose, especially in financial transactions. This awareness will help me avoid unnecessary risks and also allow me to guide my friends and family to stay safe online.
